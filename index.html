Hussein Salim

Storyteller of the In-Between Spaces

Born and raised in Kenya, Hussein Salim writes fiction rooted in heritage, healing, and the unseen bonds between past and present. His work explores the fractures of grief, the pull of ancestry, and the search for belonging in a world that often demands we choose between our roots and our wings.

With a background that spans continents and cultures, Hussein brings a unique perspective to storytelling—one that bridges the elemental forces of tradition and transformation, memory and becoming.

"I write to explore what happens when the sky stops being an escape and becomes a mirror. When the ground you thought you'd outgrown begins to call you home."

An Ancestral Journey

The Red Path follows Sekou, a flight attendant who has built a meticulous life in the sky to escape the grief of his mother's death. When a supernatural silence at 40,000 feet awakens a family compass—his mother's final gift—his carefully constructed world cracks.

Pulled back to the East African deserts he fled, Sekou must walk the mythical Red Path to uncover his mother's hidden legacy. In a journey that blurs the line between vision and reality, he confronts the parts of himself he buried and faces a choice: remain forever divided between the sky and the soil, or become whole.

"The sky doesn't save broken men. It just gives them somewhere to hide."

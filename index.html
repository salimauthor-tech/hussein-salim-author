The Work

Gold in the Fractures

Volume I of a six-volume existential cycle

He had a career that took him across the world.
A mother who was his first home.
A life that felt, for a long time, like his own.
Then all of it was gone.

Gold in the Fractures is the story of what happens after the floor gives way — the grief, the silence, the waiting, the betrayal, and the slow, difficult work of learning who you are when everything you believed about yourself has been stripped away.

Not a book about fixing yourself. About what remains when the self you constructed has finally, completely, collapsed.

The Author

The Cartographer
of Fractures

SAMIA'S is a pen name — a permanent tribute to his late mother, Samia. The apostrophe is not punctuation. It is an acknowledgment: this writing remains, in part, hers. An act of remembrance. A sadaqah jāriyah that continues beyond what words can hold.

Born in Kitui, Kenya. Former Qatar Airways Premium Cabin Crew. He has lived between the sky and the red earth — between the professional who commanded a cabin and the son who lost everything and had to learn to stand again on different ground.

His life's work is a six-volume existential cycle unified by one question: How does a human being survive the collapse of certainty and emerge not unchanged, but more true?

This is not self-help. This is not comfort for the comfortable. This is writing for those who have already tried the easy answers and found them insufficient.

What remains when everything else is stripped away?
